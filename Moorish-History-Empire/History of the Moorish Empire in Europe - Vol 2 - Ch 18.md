The passes of the Sierra Morena had been already 
occupied by the Moors when the Christian army ap¬ 
proached that range of mountains which forms the 
northern boundary of the province of Andalusia. A 
shepherd, whom superstition has exalted into a saint,

The Moorish order of battle did not differ greatly

no doubt determined the adoption of an arrangement

The attack was begun by the Castilians, who with 
headstrong impetuosity rushed forward to storm the 
enemy’s intrenchments. They were received, however,
